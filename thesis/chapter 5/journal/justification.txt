This work satisfies several PRX criteria:
1) It significantly advances the state-of-the-art approach to gravitational-wave population analyses by flexibly allowing for correlations in more than two source dimensions.
2) Successfully demonstrating this with realistic simulations and models for the first time moves the field in a new and exciting direction.
3) It establishes a stronger connection between gravitational-wave data analysis and simulations of compact-binary evolution by making the complex predictions of the latter accessible through the former.
4) It sets a landmark for fast evolving population-synthesis predictions by showing they are more difficult to distinguish than previously thought.
