As I had mentioned in my previous report to the PRL submission,

"This manuscript focuses on inferring the population properties of
binary black holes~(BBHs) using the non-parametric population model
PixelPop, the details of which are summarized by the authors in an
accompanying methods paper submitted to PRD. The method involves
pixelating the 2d subspace of specific BBH parameters into
high-resolution bins and inferring the merger rate density in each bin
from GW data using a conditional autoregressive prior. Free of the
strong assumptions that parametric population models rely on, the
authors can constrain features in the joint distribution of pairwise
BBH parameters while being driven by the data instead of a priori
considerations regarding BBH formation channels. The paper is well
written and the main results are presented in a manner accessible to a
broad audience."

While I had a major concern regarding the communications of these results to
PRL:

"While the method itself is novel and has several advantages and a few
disadvantages relative to existing non-parametric population inference
methodologies, I have a major concern regarding whether or not the
current set of results based on GWTC-3 data “substantially advances
fundamental or applied physical science”, which I elaborate below."

I am happy to recommend this communication, as is, to PRD letters section.
Congratulations to the authors for the development of this novel method and its
use in verifying existing findings from GWTC-3 with such high levels of
flexibility.
